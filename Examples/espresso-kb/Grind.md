# Grind

Grind size is the single most impactful variable in espresso brewing. It determines the surface area exposed to water and the resistance water encounters as it flows through the coffee puck. Even a tiny change in grind size can dramatically alter the taste of your shot.

## Why Grind Size Matters

The goal of espresso extraction is to dissolve desirable compounds (sugars, acids, oils) while avoiding over-extraction of bitter, astringent flavors. Grind size controls how quickly water can access those compounds.

- **Finer grind** = more surface area + tighter channels between particles. Water struggles to pass through, increasing contact time and extraction.
- **Coarser grind** = less surface area + wider channels. Water flows freely, reducing contact time and under-extracting.

## How It Changes Extraction & Shot Time

| Grind | Shot Time (for 1:2 ratio) | Extraction | Typical Flavor |
|-------|---------------------------|------------|----------------|
| Too fine | >35 seconds | Over-extracted (too much dissolved) | Bitter, harsh, dry, ashy |
| Ideal | 25–35 seconds | Balanced | Sweet, acidic, complex |
| Too coarse | <20 seconds | Under-extracted (too little dissolved) | Sour, thin, salty |

**The relationship is inverse:** finer grinds slow flow, increase time, and raise extraction yield. Coarser grinds speed flow, shorten time, and lower extraction yield.

## Dialing-In Advice

1. **Start with a consistent dose and yield.** Fix your dose (e.g., 18g) and target yield (e.g., 36g). Only adjust grind.
2. **Use the shot time as a guide.** If your 1:2 shot finishes in 20 seconds, grind finer. If it takes 40 seconds, grind coarser.
3. **Taste first, then adjust.** A sour shot needs finer grind (more extraction). A bitter shot needs coarser grind (less extraction).
4. **Make small changes.** Adjust your grinder by one or two tiny steps (or a 1–2 second change in shot time) per attempt.
5. **Check for channeling.** Uneven flow (gushing or spurting) often means grind is too fine or distribution is poor. Rely on a level tamp and WDT tool.

**Pro tip:** If your shot tastes both sour *and* bitter, your grind is likely correct but your ratio or dose is off. Adjust yield before changing grind.
